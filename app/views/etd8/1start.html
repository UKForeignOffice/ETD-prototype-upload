Apply for an emergency travel document


You can only get an emergency travel document (sometimes called an 'emergency passport') if you're abroad.


    You will need to:

  • Complete the application
  • Take a digital photo (we'll show you how)
  • Pay £100
  • Depending on where you are and when you're travelling you may need to book a courier or come into the consulate.

More information


An emergency travel document (sometimes called an ‘emergency passport’) is not the same as a normal passport. It's temporary. You travel details are printed in it. It can only be used for one trip (upto 5 destinations) and will be taken away by the border force at your final destination.


1. How it works

You can apply for an emergency travel document (sometimes called an ‘emergency passport’) if you’re abroad, need to travel and can’t get a passport in time.

There’s a different way to get a passport urgently if you’re in the UK.

You can use an emergency travel document to travel to your destination through a maximum of 5 countries. You can also normally use it to return to where you are now if you live in the country you’re applying from.

To apply you'll need:

  • an email address
  • travel plans
  • proof of residency if your final destination is outside the UK
  • a photo taken and uploaded or a physical passport sized photo

2. Eligibility

You can apply for an emergency travel document if all the following apply:

  • you’re a British national
  • you’re outside the UK
  • your passport has been lost, stolen, damaged, is full or has recently expired
  • you don’t have time to renew or replace your passport before you travel
  • you can provide proof of your travel plans, for example booking confirmations (or detailed written travel plans if you can’t book ahead)

3. Appointment or courier

You can either have it delivered to you by courier or attend an appointment at your nearest British embassy, high commission or consulate after you apply online. You can pay online or at your appointment.


4. Not a British Citizen

If you’re 16 or over and a British national but not a British citizen, contact your nearest British embassy, high commission or consulate.
